    Cousin Cost

    It is the result of adding the raw material and the cost of the direct labour.
    To process the raw material, personnel are required to handle and process the raw material directly; It is what is known as Direct Labor.

    Raw material; They are the materials that will be subjected to transformation or manufacturing operations for their physical and/or chemical change, before they can be sold as finished products.

    Production Cost

    The cost of production is expenses that an organization incurs for the development of a project. That is to say from the main material to the manufacturing process, also called "Processing cost".

    It is the result of adding prime cost plus direct manufacturing overhead plus indirect cost.

    Indirect costs

    What characterizes these costs is that they cannot be allocated directly to each of the products. It will be done only after having defined a coherent allocation criterion, which allows you to calculate your assignment to each product individually, which will guide you later when defining the final price of sale.

    Indirect costs are equal to: indirect raw material plus direct Labor plus indirect manufacturing overhead

    Distribution cost

    They refer to the expenditures (expenses and investments) that companies make to distribute their products in the market.

    Distribution costs are conformed by marketing and financing costs. They include the costs of packing, special packaging, storage, transfer of the merchandise, those generated by the payment of the personnel charged for the sale of the product, of administration and the decrease. The depletion is the loss of a physical or material good.

    Production unit Cost
    It is the average value that, to a certain volume of production, it costs to produce a unit of the product. Knowing the unit cost helps business owners determine when they will begin to profit, helping to put a price on the products with that in mind.

    Provides a dynamic overview of the relationships between revenues, costs and earnings. If the unit cost, including fixed and variable costs, is calculated as $5.00 per unit, the sale of a unit for $6.00 generates a profit of $1.00 for each sale.
    Total Cost of operation
    It refers to the entire cost of a company. The sum of all costs (Production cost and distribution cost) constitutes the total cost of the company to stay in operation.

    If a company has total costs of 50,000 dollars per month and receives revenues of 45,000 dollars in the same period, it will give losses. On the other hand, if it manages to reduce the total costs to 20,000 dollars and continues to generate revenues for 45,000, it will achieve a profit of 25,000 dollars per month. Another possibility to maximize profits is to raise sales prices, without having to change the costs
